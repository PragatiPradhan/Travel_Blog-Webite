Coorg Travel Guide

Last Updated: September, 2023

Coorg, also known as Kodagu, is a picturesque hill station located in the state of Karnataka, India. Renowned for its lush green landscapes, mist-covered hills, and coffee plantations, Coorg is a haven for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Here are some of the best places to visit in Coorg: Madikeri Fort: Located in the heart of Madikeri, the fort offers a glimpse into the region's history. The fort was built in the 17th century and provides panoramic views of the surrounding hills. Raja's Seat: A popular sunset point, Raja's Seat offers breathtaking views of the Western Ghats. It's a garden with seasonal flowers and a perfect place to relax and enjoy the scenic beauty. Abbey Falls: Nestled amidst coffee plantations and spice estates, Abbey Falls is a majestic waterfall. The sight and sound of water cascading down from a height of 70 feet make it a must-visit attraction. Namdroling Monastery (Golden Temple): Located in Bylakuppe, the Golden Temple is a significant Tibetan settlement. The monastery is known for its stunning architecture, vibrant paintings, and a peaceful atmosphere. Dubare Elephant Camp: Situated on the banks of the Cauvery River, this camp provides a unique opportunity to interact with elephants. Visitors can participate in activities like elephant bathing and feeding. Talakaveri: It is the origin of the River Kaveri and is located on the Brahmagiri Hill. The panoramic views of the surrounding hills and the temple dedicated to Lord Agastheeswara make it a spiritually significant spot. Iruppu Falls: Iruppu Falls is another enchanting waterfall in Coorg, surrounded by lush greenery. It is believed to have medicinal properties, and a visit here is not only visually pleasing but also culturally significant. Tadiandamol Peak: For trekking enthusiasts, Tadiandamol, the highest peak in Coorg, offers an exhilarating trek with stunning views of the Western Ghats. Coffee Plantations: Coorg is famous for its coffee plantations. Visitors can take guided tours to learn about the coffee-making process, from planting to harvesting. Mandalpatti Viewpoint: For those seeking panoramic views of the Western Ghats, Mandalpatti is a must-visit. The viewpoint is accessible by a thrilling jeep ride and offers breathtaking vistas. Coorg, with its diverse attractions, is a destination that appeals to nature lovers, history enthusiasts, and adventure seekers alike. The pleasant climate and warm hospitality of the locals add to the charm of this beautiful hill station in southern India.
